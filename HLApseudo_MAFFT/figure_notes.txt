Figure notes — hla_shared_fraction.pdf/png
==========================================

HLA-A
One large contiguous block — essentially the entire CDS region (positions ~50–900) is
≥90% shared with at least one of H, J, L, P, V. Then a hard drop to zero. That cliff
at ~900 bp is the 3'UTR becoming unique. This is precisely the sequence space that
kallisto/Salmon exploit for their discriminating k-mers, and exactly what is lost when
CDS-only references are used.

HLA-DRB1
Similar shape but with interior structure — the valleys around positions 200 and 500–600
likely correspond to exon boundaries or regions where the pseudogene transcript models
have gaps (partial annotation). The 66% overall is comparable to HLA-A, but the profile
is less monolithic, which matters: if a study uses a short 3'UTR-anchored protocol, those
interior unique islands will not be captured.

HLA-B
A completely different pattern — fragmented peaks, none reaching 100%, overall only 15%.
The B/C-clade pseudogenes (K, N, T, U, W) are clearly far more degenerated than the
A-clade ones. This quantitatively explains why HLA-B is somewhat less affected by the
mappability artifact in practice, though the peaks still reach 70–80% locally, which is
enough to lose reads in those windows.
